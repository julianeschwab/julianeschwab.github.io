About me

My name is Juliane Schwab, I am a PhD candidate in the DFG-funded Research Training Group Computational Cognition at the Institute of Cognitive Science in Osnabr√ºck, Germany. I am also employed at the Department of English and American Studies at Humboldt University of Berlin. My PhD was supervised by Prof. Dr. Mingya Liu (Humboldt University of Berlin) and Prof. Dr. Jutta L. Mueller (University of Vienna). In my project, I investigated the semantics, pragmatics, and processing of polarity items using psycho- and neurolinguistic methods.

In my research, I aim for an interdisciplinary perspective, combining methods and insights from theoretical linguistics, psycho- and neurolinguistics, and Bayesian statistical modelling to elucidate how natural language is used and processed, and to characterize language from a dynamic interactive perspective between speaker(s), listener(s) and context in a wide sense.

I am broadly interested in the semantics/pragmatics interface, probabilistic pragmatic modelling, and cognitive mechanisms involved in language processing.
